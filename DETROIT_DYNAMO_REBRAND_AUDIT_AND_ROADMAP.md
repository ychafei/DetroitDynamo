## 2. Main Issues Found

- The earlier preview covered Training, Academy, FC, Booking, Results, About, and Brand, but it did not yet cover the full requested public-site structure for Youth Club, Senior Men, Senior Women, Tryouts, Teams, Schedule & Results, Camps & Clinics, Sponsors, and Contact.
- The current worktree previously contained broad Detroit Dynamo theme edits outside the isolated preview shell. Focused isolation passes restored the root metadata/PWA manifest, shared navbar/footer branding, global theme tokens, home/about/auth/legal/current LCFC/team copy, and transactional email footers to the current LC Training / LCFC / Les Chèvres shell while preserving subtle Dynamo preview entry points.
- Some compatibility route names remain legacy, such as `/lcfc` and `/admin/lcfc`. These should stay until a formal redirect/promotion plan is approved.
- Existing transactional/legal content and support references still include legacy operational details such as `support@lctrainings.com`. Those should be migrated only when the legal/payment/support transition is approved.
- The Appwrite backend has existing collections for coaches, sessions, pricing, players, LCFC content, gallery, sponsors, and news, but not the full Detroit Dynamo club operating model yet.

## 3. Implementation Plan

Recommended order:
1. Preserve existing booking, auth, payments, blog, admin, coach, and team workflows.
2. Complete the isolated `/detroit-dynamo/*` public preview site.
3. Use preview lead forms to validate the Youth Club, Senior Men, Senior Women, Tryouts, Camps, Sponsor, and Contact flows.
4. Provision Appwrite collections for the Detroit Dynamo data model after form/field requirements are accepted.
5. Build the admin dashboard modules behind role guards.
6. Confirm league memberships, staff, facilities, sponsor packages, pricing, waivers, and payment products before publishing claims or checkout flows.
7. Promote the Detroit Dynamo shell over the current site only after redirect, SEO, legal, payment, support, and content migration are approved.
